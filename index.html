the steganography tool

How it works:

Step 1: Encode Each Pixel

We alter the Least Significant Bit (see info section below) to include more information.

Step 2: Alter the Picture

Since we altered something so small, it is undetectable to the human eye.

Step 3: Decode the Hidden Information

We can see what we've altered in the LSB and use that to decode the message.

About Steganography:

Steganography is the process of concealing data inside images, using various techniques, such as only looking at the LSB or using the alpha channel of an image. This practice was first recorded in 1499 by Johannes Trithemius, who hid messages in images, lists and other words.

The location of the binary integer that determines whether the number is even or odd is called the least significant bit (LSB).
